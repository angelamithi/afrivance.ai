The Problem We Solve

"It wasn't me" shouldn't be the end of the conversation.

When a shipment goes missing or arrives damaged, the usual record is a spreadsheet or a notebook — something anyone along the chain could have changed after the fact. That means disputes drag on, trust breaks down, and nobody can say for certain who actually had the goods when something went wrong.

The Old Way

  • Records are kept in spreadsheets or paper logs that can be edited after the fact
  • When something goes wrong, it's one party's word against another's
  • Customers and partners have no way to independently verify what happened
  • Disputes take weeks to resolve, if they ever do

With Manifest

  • Every handover is recorded permanently — nobody can quietly change the record
  • You can see exactly who had the shipment at every step of the journey
  • Anyone — a customer, an auditor, a partner — can check the history themselves
  • If something is disputed, it's flagged immediately and locked until resolved
How It Works

Four simple steps, every shipment

No complicated setup. Each shipment moves through the same trusted process from start to finish.

1

Shipment is created

A digital record is created the moment a shipment starts its journey, noting what it is and who has it first.

2

Every stop is logged

As the shipment moves — warehouse, transit point, delivery — each stop is recorded with a timestamp, permanently.

3

Handovers are recorded

When responsibility passes from one party to the next, that handover is captured as a verified event — not just a note.

4

Anyone can check it

The full journey can be viewed by anyone with the shipment ID — no special access or technical knowledge needed.
